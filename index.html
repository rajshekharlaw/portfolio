Some careers follow a straight line. Mine took a deliberate turn. From advising FinTech startups on regulatory compliance to shaping the policies that govern them. Today, I work at the intersection of law, legislation, and democratic governance, driven by a simple belief: policy is not just drafted, it is debated, negotiated, and ultimately, lived.

My journey through India's leading law firms, Khaitan & Co and Nishith Desai Associates, gave me a front row seat to how regulations shape markets. But it also revealed a deeper truth: the real action happens before the law reaches the statute books. That realization led me from corporate practice to the Parliament and Legislative Affairs Division, where I now contribute to the very process of lawmaking.

Legislation is as much about persuasion and process as it is about drafting. The best policies are those that survive scrutiny beyond conference rooms.

From co-authoring White Papers for Y20 India (the official youth engagement group of G20) to being selected among 26 leaders nationwide for the Konrad Adenauer Stiftung Rising Leaders Programme, I have engaged with governance at both national and international levels. My work with the Commonwealth Youth Council and the Indian Youth Diplomacy Forum continues this mission, bridging policy research with diplomatic action.

Why I Left Corporate Law

After years of advising clients on RBI regulations, data privacy frameworks, and FinTech compliance, I found myself increasingly drawn to a different question: Who writes these rules, and how?

Corporate law taught me to navigate regulations. Policy work lets me help shape them. It is the difference between reading the playbook and being in the room where it is written.

The transition was not accidental. It was built through deliberate choices: joining Y20 India during India's G20 presidency, completing leadership training with Konrad Adenauer Stiftung, and engaging with governance dialogues across ASEAN and the Commonwealth.

Today, I bring a rare combination to policy research: the precision of legal training and the strategic thinking of political engagement.
